Operating System

How I think about building software.

Strong portfolios do not just list tools. They reveal judgment. These are the defaults I bring into design reviews, incident calls, and AI product work.

01

Make state explicit.

Most production bugs hide in implicit state: retries, duplicate webhooks, stale UI, hidden auth assumptions. I prefer designs where ownership, lifecycle, and failure modes are visible.

02

Evaluate AI like software.

RAG quality is not vibes. Chunking, retrieval, reranking, prompts, fallbacks, and latency all need testable hypotheses and human-readable traces.

03

Boring infra wins.

Serverless, queues, DynamoDB conditions, CDK/Terraform, logs, alarms, and small blast radii beat clever architectures that cannot be operated at 2 AM.

04

UX is part of reliability.

If users cannot understand what the system did, they will invent stories. Good interfaces expose status, history, retry paths, ownership, and confidence.
